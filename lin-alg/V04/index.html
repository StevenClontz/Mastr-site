Example 1

V4 - Subspaces (ver. 1)

Consider the following two sets of Euclidean vectors:

\[U=\left\{ \left[\begin{array}{r} x \\ y \\ z \\ w \end{array}\right] \hspace{0.2em}\middle|\hspace{0.2em} 5 \, x = z^{2} - 4 \, w + 4 \, y \right\} \hspace{2em} W=\left\{ \left[\begin{array}{r} x \\ y \\ z \\ w \end{array}\right] \hspace{0.2em}\middle|\hspace{0.2em} 5 \, x + 4 \, y + 2 \, z = 6 \, w \right\}.\]

Explain why one of these sets is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^4\), and why the other is not.

Answer.

\(W\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^4\) and \(U\) is not.
