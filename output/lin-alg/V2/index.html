Example 1

V2 - Linear Combinations (ver. 1)

Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\), \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 4 \\ -8 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\), and \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 8 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{array}\right]\) is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 6 \\ 1 \\ -2 \\ -10 \end{array}\right]\), \(\left[\begin{array}{r} 8 \\ 4 \\ -8 \\ -8 \end{array}\right]\), and \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -9 \\ -6 \\ -1 \\ -7 \end{array}\right]\).
