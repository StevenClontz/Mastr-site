E1 - Augmented Matrices

Example 1

E1 - Augmented Matrices (ver. 1)

Give an augmented matrix that represents \begin{align*} x_{1} + x_{2} - 6 \, x_{3} &= 8 \\ x_{2} - 5 \, x_{3} &= 6 \\ -2 \, x_{1} + x_{2} + 4 \, x_{3} &= -5 \\ -x_{2} + 2 \, x_{3} &= -3 \\ \end{align*}

Answer.

\[\left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 & 1 & -6 & 8 \\ 0 & 1 & -5 & 6 \\ -2 & 1 & 4 & -5 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 & -3 \end{array}\right]\]

Give an augmented matrix that represents \begin{align*} -x_{2} - x_{3} - 2 \, x_{4} &= 3 \\ 2 \, x_{1} - x_{2} - 3 \, x_{3} - 8 \, x_{4} &= -5 \\ -x_{1} + x_{3} + 3 \, x_{4} &= 4 \\ \end{align*}

Answer.

\[\left[\begin{array}{rrrr|r} 0 & -1 & -1 & -2 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 & -3 & -8 & -5 \\ -1 & 0 & 1 & 3 & 4 \end{array}\right]\]

Example 50

E1 - Augmented Matrices (ver. 50)

Give an augmented matrix that represents \begin{align*} 4 \, x_{1} - 8 \, x_{2} + 4 \, x_{3} &= 0 \\ 2 \, x_{1} - 3 \, x_{2} + x_{3} &= 1 \\ -x_{1} + 7 \, x_{2} - 6 \, x_{3} &= 5 \\ -x_{1} + 2 \, x_{2} - x_{3} &= 0 \\ \end{align*}

Answer.

\[\left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 4 & -8 & 4 & 0 \\ 2 & -3 & 1 & 1 \\ -1 & 7 & -6 & 5 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 & 0 \end{array}\right]\]
