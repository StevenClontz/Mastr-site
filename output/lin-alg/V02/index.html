Explain why the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 20 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is or is not a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).

Answer.

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} 20 \\ -5 \\ -4 \\ -5 \end{array}\right]\) is a linear combination of the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{c} -5 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\).
