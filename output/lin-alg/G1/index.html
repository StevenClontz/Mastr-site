G1 - Row operations and matrices

Example 1

G1 - Row operations and matrices (ver. 1)

(a) Give a \(3\times 3\) matrix \(P\) that may be used to perform the row operation \({R_3} \to -2 \, {R_1} + {R_3}\).

(b) Give a \(3\times 3\) matrix \(Q\) that may be used to perform the row operation \({R_3} \to -4 \, {R_3}\).

(c) Suppose \(A\) is a \(3\times 3\) matrix with determinant \(5\). Use matrix multiplication to describe the matrix obtained by applying \({R_3} \to -2 \, {R_1} + {R_3}\) and then \({R_3} \to -4 \, {R_3}\) to \(A\). Then explain how to find its determinant.

Answer.

(a) \(\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\)

(b) \(\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -4 \end{array}\right]\)

(c) \(-20\)
