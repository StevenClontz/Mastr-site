Explain why the vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) span or don't span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

Answer.

The vectors \(\left[\begin{array}{r} -3 \\ -3 \\ -1 \\ -4 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 4 \\ 2 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -5 \\ -1 \end{array}\right] , \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 4 \\ 3 \\ 3 \end{array}\right] , \text{ and } \left[\begin{array}{r} -4 \\ 3 \\ -1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\) do span \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
